Housing Affordability in the Des Moines Metropolitan Area

Housing Burden

According to the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, families who pay more than 30 percent of their income for housing are considered cost burdened and may have difficulty affording necessities such as food, clothing, transportation, and medical care. The figure below shows the percentage of median household incomes spent on housing for each city in the Des Moines Metropolitan Area. This percentage was calculated by dividing the median household income by 12, to get the median monthly household income. Then the median monthly household income was divided by the median monthly housing cost in that city. This percentage is only based on the median values provided by the 2010 U.S. Census, so actual percentages may not be exact. In the Des Moines Metropolitan Area, only one city (Indianola) has more than 30% percent of their median household income spent on housing. The principle city in the metropolitan area, Des Moines, is very close to 30% of their median household income being spent on housing. In general, not many households in the Des Moines Metropolitan Area face housing burden, which shows that housing in the Des Moines Metropolitan Area is affordable.

Vacancy Rates

The national rental vacancy rate is 6.9%. In the Des Moines Metropolitan Area, Johnston and Altoona have rental vacancy rates below the national average which could mean that there is a high demand for rental housing and too small of a supply. This can cause prices to be higher, however the percentage of median household income spent on housing is not particularly high in those cities, so it is not causing too much of a problem. The national homeowner vacancy rate is 1.8%. In the Des Moines Metropolitan Area, Des Moines, West Des Moines, Waukee, Urbandale, Ankeny, and Altoona have homeowner vacancy rates higher than the national average which can mean that there is not as high of a demand for owner-occupied housing than the supply which can lead to lower housing prices over time.
